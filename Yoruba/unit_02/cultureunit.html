Some Expectations in making introductions

Greetings are very important amongst the Yoruba. There is also the expectation of a significant level of cordiality in any interaction amongst the people. Part of this geniality is the process of getting introduced to one another. As with greetings, the older individual is expected to be the one being ‘introduced’ and would be expected to do most of the ‘questioning’ or request for information. Unlike in the exchange of greetings however, the younger person is free to make enquiries as he or she attempts to become acquainted with the older person. Some of the information that can be sought in the introduction include: names, place of origin, residency, home town, address, occupation, telephone number and nationality among other things. Such introductions, more often than not, also includes enquiries about the respondent’s family. The key exception to this flexibility is enquiring about someone’s age. A younger person will be considered impolite if he or she enquires from an older person about their age. In fact, enquiries about age are not common place among the Yoruba.

 

Making some simple enquiries:

Information being sought         Ibeere / Enquiry

Orúkv (name)                         Kí ni orúkv rc?                       (to one person or an age mate)

                        Kí ni orúkv yín?           (to many or to an older person): Answer                    Oruko mi ni _________

            Answer                        Mo wá láti __________

            Answer                        Mo n gbe ni _________
