Unit 15 Siasa

Political rallies and associated activities, like singing, dancing, a political speech, or poetry reading, bear some marks of the culture of the people. Although these have every marker of political discourse, they rely on the way in which the people participate. By mixing cultural beliefs on how to celebrate a social occasion, these political events usually turn out to be a day of festivity which attracts people of all walks of life. Politicians, like all politicians in the world, seize this opportunity to push forward their political agendas knowing that the people are having a good time. Thus, speeches which praise key political leaders or diminish those in opposition are the characteristic of such political rallies. At such rallies, important policies are announced, taking advantage of the large audience and the media who will spread the word to those who did not attend the rally.

Involving the youth is another strategy of winning members and the hearts of the people. Parents with children participating in a political event tend to be party loyalists who will vote for the party in local and national elections. The reading of poetry is a tradition that has deep roots in the Swahili culture. Its importance in the culture of the Swahili people resides in the history of poetry and the functions of poetry in the language. The history of poetry is as old as the use of the language on the coastal strip of East Africa. There are many reasons for composing and using poems. Often poems are read or sang in praise of someone, to chastise, to talk about happy and sad events, and so forth. The poem read on the video was composed by the Party Youth League and was intended to do two main things: to praise the leadership of the party and to speak directly to the public about social and political philosophies that appeal to the different groups of political persuasion.

Poems are considered metaphorical, the preferred language in politics. The skill acquired in writing and reading poems is revered by many. The rhythmic structure attracts listeners, and the compressed meaning of the message leaves room for the audience to create their own interpretations.
