B.  The Education System

It used to be easy to describe the education system in East Africa as unique and specific to the country in question. Nowadays, however, much of the education system looks up to the western world in its curriculum and methodology. But, there are some specifics that are not found in the western system. For example, at the elementary level, students are expected to help with keeping their school clean and still assemble before classes for important announcements. The level of discipline is also very high, a much needed asset considering that classes are rather large with between 100 and 150 students. Education is free with minimal contribution from parents for supplies and minor school repairs. The schools can survive on a bare minimum of resources. As such, students have high regard for the written word and education is considered a privilege and not a right. While elementary education is compulsory, very few have the opportunity to receive secondary or university education. Thus, less than one percent of all those who graduate at any given level continue beyond that level.  

The emergence of TV and other technological innovations continues to transform society and consequently the quality of education. Students in many of the secondary schools and universities have adapted to these innovations. Minor discrepancies can be noted but they all depend on the resources available to individual schools. As such, it is advisable for the learners who are interested in exploring about the education system to consult the literature on the specifics.
