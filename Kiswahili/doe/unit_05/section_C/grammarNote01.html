About Nouns in this unit:
Noun Group: Ki - Vi
Singular Plural Gloss
kitu vitu ‘thing/things’
kiungo viungo ‘spice/spices’

Nouns in this group were discussed in unit 3 lesson 4 and in unit 4. Also review grammar, unit 1, lesson 2 and Unit 3, lesson 1 and 4 where we briefly mentioned that some nouns in this group that refer to animals behave differently with respect to agreement. An example is kijana ‘youth’ whose plural is vijana ‘youths’ and whose agreement follows from that used with nouns that refer to humans and animals. Thus:

Singular Gloss Plural Gloss
kijana wa kwanza ‘the first youth’ vijana wa kesho ‘tomorrow’s youth’
kijana mzuri ‘a good youth’ vijana wazuri ‘good youths’
kijana wangu ‘my youth’ vijana wangu ‘my youths’
kijana anasoma ‘the youth is reading’ vijana wanasoma ‘the youth are reading’
