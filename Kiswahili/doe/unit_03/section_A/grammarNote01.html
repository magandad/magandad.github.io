This group of nouns is also identified as class 1 & 2 nouns. Class 1 refers to the singular nouns and class 2, their plurals. The second group below does not bear any distinguishing markers for singular or plural. Speakers use context to disambiguate.

no prefix (singular) no prefix (plural)
kaka 'brother' kaka 'brothers'
dada 'sister' dada 'sisters'
baba 'father' baba 'fathers'
mama 'mother' mama 'mothers'

These nouns are often included in a class with nouns whose singular and plural forms are not distinguishable. The class is often referred to as class 9 & 10 nouns (to be discussed later). However, as it will become evident later, nouns like baba, mama, dada, and other nouns referring to animals behave like the nouns designated class 1 & 2 and not like nouns in class 9 & 10 in which they are often listed in different grammars of Kiswahili. One noun which refers to living beings but remains in class 9&10 is familia 'family'.

Review Adjectives in Unit 1 including examples showing agreement with selected nouns.

Review the verb have in unit 1 then consider kuna 'there is/are' as used in this example in the text:
Katika familia ya bwana Ahmed kuna watu watano.
In Mr. Ayub's family, there are five people.

Ku- is used to indicate that the noun in reference describes a location. Thus the translation has the preposition in making Mr. Ayub's family to be the place where the five members reside.
