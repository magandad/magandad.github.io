Utadumani: Cultural Notes

The concept of time is understood differently from the west. Speakers use natural instincts to determine the time of day and often times rely on solar movement. For example, the rising sun mark the morning hours while the overhead position of the sun marks the beginning of the afternoon. Evening is marked by sunset. In some cases too, speakers use the shadow (any type including human) to determine the time of the day. The middle of the day is marked by the absence of a shadow (because the sun is overhead). Also, one knows the end of the day is closer by the length of the shadow the closer the day is to end.

In telling time we use the same numerical number1-12 for hours and up to 59 for minutes. Telling time in Kiswahili begins with the first hour of daylight to the twelfth hour. Thus, speakers would use the following terms in telling time:
