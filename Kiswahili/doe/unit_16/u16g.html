B. Review the passive
(i) Matokeo ya utafiti yanatumiwa na idara mbalimbali.
'Research results are used by different departments.'

(ii) Kozi hii inafadhiliwa na SADC.
'This course is funded by SADC.'

Basically the passive suffix is w, but the consonant versus vowel ending of the verb root (before the final vowel ending -a) will dictate whether anything more than w needs to be included. The examples above show that when a verb ends in a consonant, w is added. Here are additional rules to remember.

(i) Verbs ending with [ia or ea] insert w between them.

saidia 'help' > saidi-w-a 'be helped.'
angalia 'look' > angali-w-a 'be looked at/after'
tumia 'use' > tumi-w-a 'be used'
pokea 'receive' > poke-w-a 'be received'
pea (-pa) 'give' > pe-w-a 'be given'

(ii) Verbs ending in [aa, oa, ua]: insert [l+i/e+w] between them.
zaa 'give birth' > za-liw-a 'be born'
toa 'give' > to-lew-a 'be given, be taken out,
nunua 'buy' > nunu-liw-a 'be bought'

(iii) Verbs of non-Bantu origin (often end with i or u): change final vowel to -i, add w and final vowel -a. and if with double , add li or le.

jaribu 'try' > jarib-iw-a 'be tried'
hitaji 'need' > hitaj-iw-a 'be needed'
kubali 'agree' > kubal-iw-a 'be accepted'
sahau 'forget' > sahau-liw-a 'be forgotten'

A verb in this group ending with -e- behaves differently.

samehe 'forgive' > samehe-w-a 'be forgiven'

(iv) Monosyllabic verb roots: must be learned individually since only three can take an object.

(ku)la (to) eat' > l-iw-a 'be eaten'
(ku)nywa (to) drink' > nyw-iw-a 'be drunk up'
(ku)pa (to) give > p-ew-a 'be given'

C. Review -ki- (conditional tense) See Unit 14 for a discussion of the -ki- continuous tense.

The Conditional marker -ki- is also considered a conditional tense that is used to express simple conditions. Its use is different from that discussed in Unit 13 that expresses an action going on while another has been completed. The conditional -ki- expresses a conditional meaning. For example:

Ukitembelea vijijini utaona tofauti........
'if you visit the villages, you will see a difference.....'

Often, the condition is introduced by kama which we discussed earlier as having the function of expressing a condition. The use of kama with -ki- reinforces the condition. Thus (i) and (ii) below do not have a meaning change. However, the condition in (ii) is reinforced compared to (i).

(i) Ukitembelea vijijini, utaona watu wengi.
'If you visit the villages, you will see many people.'

(ii) Kama ukitembelea vijijini, utaona watu wengi.'
'Should you visit the villages, you will see many people.'

(i) is considered a less tentative condition, (ii)is more definitive meaning 'if and only if'
