A. Review of baada ya, kabla ya, badala ya

These adverbs of time and substitution baada ya, 'after' kabla ya, 'before' and badala ya, 'instead of.') require the following verb to take the infinitive marker ku- 'to'.

kabla ya kuenda kazini 'before going to work'

baada ya kuenda kazini 'after going to work'

badala ya kuenda kazini 'instead of going to work'

Kabla ya kuenda kazini, ninakula chamshakinywa.
'Before I go to work, I eat breakfast.'

Baada ya kuenda kazini, mama anapika chakula cha jioni.
'After going to work, mother cooks the evening meal.'

Badala ya kuenda kazini, mama analea watoto.
'Instead of going to work, mother raises the children.'

It is, therefore, ungrammatical to use the finite form of the verb with a subject prefix and tense marking.

A related adverb of time is baadaye 'afterwards/thereafter'. This is the same form as baada ya only that it is the contracted form of baada yake
>baadaye. It is also used to mean 'then' when it is used with the narrative form -ka-. For example:

Alipika na baadaye akaenda kumwona mama yake,.
'She cooked and then (thereafter) went to see her mother.'

B. Review object markers for all classes.

Object markers can be used for anaphoric (to refer to a previously mentioned noun) purposes. For examples:

(i) Anatwanga kisamvu. Baada ya kukitwanga------- anakipelekea------, anakipika.
'She is pounding cassava leaves. After pounding them, she takes it ---she cooks it.'

ii) Anatayarisha nazi. Kwanza anaipara -----, anaipasua, -----anaikuna)
'She prepares the coconut. First she removes its husks, she breaks it, she grates it.'

In (i) ki is an object marker that is used anaphorically to refer to kisamvu. By the same token, in (ii), I is an objectmarker making anaphoric reference to nazi.

C. Review of the narrative -na-

In narratives, the usual tense found is -ka- (see Unit 13). This marks actions done in a sequence in the past. However, one can find narrative -na- in descriptions of sequenced actions that are taking place as the speaker narrates them. This is typical in sports casting. For example:

Juma ana mpira, anapiga kona, anampasia Soto, Soto anao, anampasia Kaiza, Kaiza anampasia Tumbo, Tumbo anao, anao, ---go-o-o-o-l.

'Juma has the ball, he turns a corner, he passes it to Soto, Soto has it (the ball), he passes it to Kaiza, Kaiza passes it to Tumbo, (Tumbo) has it, Tumbo has it, goal!!!!

This can be contrasted with the narrative -ka- of story telling (narrating a past story):

Juma alikuwa na mpira, akapiga kona, akampasia Soto, Soto akapasia Kaiza, Kaiza akampasia Tumbo, Tumbo akafunga goli.

'Juma took the ball, he turned a corner, he passed it to Soto, Soto passed it to Kaiza, Kaiza passed it to Tumbo, and then Tumbo scored a goal"
