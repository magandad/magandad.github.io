What is the difference between nipe and niandikie?

The difference is that we have in the verb andika ‘write’ an –i- right before the final vowel (-a which changed to –e because the mood changed from indicative to subjunctive). This –I- is know as the applied suffix whose function is similar to that a preposition in English. Here –i- represents ‘to’. The shape chages depending on the vowel in the verb root. A verb root is the part of the verb without the final vowel For example andik-a. The vowels in the root are a and i. The preposition form for this verb is therefore –i-. Other verb forms that would be affected similarly would be those with the vowel u. For example:   vuta  ‘pull’ = nivutie ‘pull for/towards me’

Suposing we have the verb soma ‘read’. The verb root has the vowel o. In that case the the prepositional form will be –e-. Thus: nisomee ‘read to/for me’ Other verbs that would affected similarly are those with the vowel e. For example: leta ‘bring’ = niletee.

Note that the prepositional form remains constant regardless of the mood (i.e. indicative negative, or subjunctive). We will come back to this and other prepositional forms in later units. Here as a few start-up examples:

[a, i, u] verb-roots

                  anz-a   ‘start’  > anz-i-a    ‘start for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

                  pik-a    ‘cook’   > pik-i-a     ‘cook for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

                  uz-a     ‘sell’     > uz-i-a              ‘sell for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

                  nunu-a ‘buy’    > nunu-li-a ‘buy for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

[e, o] verb-roots

                  let-a     ‘bring’ > let-e-a     ‘bring for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

                  tok-a     ‘appear’ > tok-e-a ‘appear for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

                  to-a      ‘give’     > to-le-a  ‘give for/on behalf of/at/in/on’

The infinitive

The infinitive form is formed by adding the prefix ku- to a verb stem as in

ku-niandikia ‘to write to me’, kutumia ‘to use’ kwenda (kuenda) ‘to go’

Other examples:

      kuandika          ‘to write’

      kunywa            ‘to drink’

      kuzaliwa          ‘to be born’

      kufundisha            ‘to teach’

      kujifunza         ‘to learn’

      kupika             ‘to cook’

      kula                 ‘to eat’

     kusafisha          ‘to wash’

     kumaliza           ‘to finish/complete’

      Ninapenda kusoma.      ‘I like to read/study.’

      Ninataka kusoma.         ‘I want to read.’

      Ninaweza kusoma. ‘I can/am able to read.’

The infinitive may also be used to translate as a gerund (verbal noun). This may be used as in: Kufundisha watoto ni kazi ya mama . ‘Teaching children is a mother’s job’
